Preserve Traffic, Ranch HVAC Loads, and 290 Logistics

Dripping Springs stretches along US 290 west of Austin, with Hamilton Pool Road diving south toward Travis County’s Hamilton Pool Preserve—a 50-foot limestone waterfall preserve that draws reservation-only crowds and clogged farm-to-market roads on peak weekends. Those same roads are how our trucks reach estates and newer subdivisions around Ranch Road 12 and Fitzhugh.

Hill Country acreage here differs from Round Rock production homes: longer line sets, metal buildings with ductless add-ons, radiant-hot tin roofs over shops, and condensers baking on west-facing pads with little tree cover. Wedding venue calendar weekends create Friday–Sunday AC failures at the same time roads are slowest.

For Dripping Springs emergencies we still target same-day visits, typically within about 1–2 hours when crews are west of Austin—then diagnose sun-heavy loads correctly so you don’t get an oversized system that short-cycles on cooler nights.
