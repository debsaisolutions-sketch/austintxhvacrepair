River Humidity, Campus Density, Outer-Radius Honesty

San Marcos sits on I-35 south of Kyle where the San Marcos River springs draw tubers to City Park and Rio Vista while Texas State University packs apartments, rentals, and older homes around campus. Move-in August is a peak AC month: coils neglected between leases freeze, and landlords call the minute orientation weekend hits 100°.

The historic Hays County Courthouse Square, outlet mall traffic off I-35, and Aquarena / Meadows Center eco-tourism create a weekend rhythm unlike northern suburbs. River-adjacent humidity makes condensate management as important as raw BTUs—oversized systems from Austin’s hillier westside playbooks leave dorm-area rentals clammy.

Because San Marcos sits at the outer edge of our real service radius, we don’t advertise a 1–2 hour ETA we can’t reliably hit when crews are in Round Rock or Cedar Park. For emergency no-cool calls here, we’ll get to you within a few hours—often same day—then diagnose and repair with the same standards we use closer in. Planned maintenance and replacements can be booked with clearer appointment windows.
