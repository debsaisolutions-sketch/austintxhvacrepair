Square Shade vs. Sun City Sun

Georgetown’s historic Williamson County Courthouse Square still sets the city’s identity: limestone storefronts, deep canopy that keeps downtown cooler than new subdivisions sprawling toward Round Rock. Two branches of the San Gabriel River and Rivery Park paths add humidity pockets after spring floods that older attic units weren’t sealed against.

Sun City Texas and other active-adult communities bring a different load profile—longer thermostat setbacks, precise humidity comfort expectations, and equipment aging on a predictable maintenance calendar. Meanwhile Wolf Ranch and other I-35-adjacent builds bake in afternoon sun with HOA plantings that pack coil fins with mulch dust.

We route around Square festival weekends and Christmas Stroll density. Most Georgetown emergency calls on the I-35 side land within about 1–2 hours when northern crews are rolling.
